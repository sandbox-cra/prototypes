CRA account help - Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

Backup multi-factor authentication being introduced

When signing in to your CRA account, you will be prompted to add a backup multi-factor authentication option if you do not already have one.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) increases the security of your CRA account by requiring you to enter a one-time passcode every time you sign in. MFA is mandatory for all CRA account users.

  • MFA options

MFA options

When you register for a CRA account, you will be prompted to enroll in 2 of the following 3 MFA options to get your one-time passcodes:

  • Third-party authenticator app: Use a mobile app that generates passcodes
  • Passcode grid: Save or print a passcode grid the CRA generates
  • Phone: Get passcodes sent to you by text or phone call
 Authenticator app

    tab test

  1. Get an authenticator app
    Search for and download a free third-party authenticator app from a reputable app store
    How to choose a reputable authenticator app

    Most reputable, standard authenticator apps should be able to read the QR code we provide. To make sure the app you are downloading is reputable:

    • Download only from official app stores (such as Google Play, the Microsoft Store, or the Apple App Store)
    • Read the privacy agreement before you download
    • Read the reviews
    • Check the number of downloads (a low download count may be a red flag)

    You can also learn about How to recognize a scam.

  2. Set up the app to use with your CRA account (one time only)
    When you are setting up this MFA option for your CRA account, either:

    Scan the provided QR code with a mobile device when prompted Option 1 of 2

    Manually enter the setup key the CRA provides to you into the app Option 2 of 2

  3. Get your passcode in the authenticator app
    Every time you sign in to your CRA account and are prompted for a passcode, open your authenticator app to get the 6-digit code. For security, the app will generate a new passcode every 30 seconds.
 Passcode grid

The passcode grid is a table made up of numbered rows and lettered columns, similar to a Bingo card.

  1. Get your passcode grid
    When you enroll with this option, the CRA will generate a unique passcode grid for you
  2. Save or print your grid
    You will use this same grid every time you sign in for the next 18 months
  3. Use the grid to get your passcode
    Whenever you sign in to your CRA account, we will ask for row and column combinations from your grid (for example, B,1; A,3) to create your one-time passcode. We will ask for 3 of these combinations each time you sign in.
  4. Generate a new grid every 18 months
    Your grid expires every 18 months. Before it expires, you can:
    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. On your Welcome page select Security settings
    3. Select Multi-factor authentication and generate a new grid
    4. Save or print your updated passcode grid

    If your passcode has already expired, you can still use it to sign in. You will then get a new one to save or print.

    5. If you lose your passcode grid, you will still be able to sign in if you have added the phone or authenticator app option for MFA. If you have not, you will need to contact us.

 Phone

If you choose to enroll with the phone option, you will need to provide at least one cell or landline number.

Using VoIP

You can use a VoIP service with multi-factor authentication. However, some VoIP services may not be compatible with MFA. If you can’t get the one-time passcode by text, choose the “Call me” delivery method.

Using an international phone number

You can receive your passcode to phone numbers based within North American countries that participate in the North American Numbering Plan (countries that an individual can call from Canada by dialing 1 + 10 digits). The phone number must be supplied by telephone providers.

Phone numbers in the following countries can receive a passcode:

  • American Samoa
  • Anguilla
  • Antigua and Barbuda
  • Bahamas
  • Barbados
  • Bermuda
  • British Virgin Islands
  • Canada
  • Cayman Islands

Alternately, you can enroll with the passcode grid or third-party authenticator app options which do not need access to a phone at all.

When you sign in, you can choose to receive your one-time passcode by:

  • Text message Option 1 of 2

    Open the text message first, then use the passcode from the body of the message.

    The CRA does not charge for this service, however your provider may charge standard message and data rates.

  • Phone call Option 2 of 2

    Your passcode will be verbally told to you in an automated message from a toll-free number.

If you did not receive your one-time passcode, you can:

  • Ask for it to be resent or sent to a different phone number that you have already enrolled with
  • Try the Call me option instead of Text me if you are using a VoIP service
  • Use one of the other two MFA options if you have them set up

If you still can't receive your one-time passcode, you will need to contact us.

Why we prompt you to enroll in 2 or more MFA options

Enrolling in multiple MFA options ensures that you can still access your CRA account if you change your phone number, misplace your passcode grid, or delete the third-party authenticator app.

If your account becomes locked

If your account becomes locked due to entering the wrong one-time passcode too many times, you will be temporarily locked out for 30 minutes. After that period, you can try again or use another method you are enrolled with. If this happens 3 times, you will be permanently locked out and will need to contact us.

Keep your passcode secure

You should never share your one-time passcode with anyone, including anyone calling or texting you and asking for it. The CRA will never call and ask you for your one-time passcode over the phone.

However, if you call us, you can choose to verify your identity during the call by providing your one-time passcode to:

  • Our Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system
  • The CRA service representative

You can also learn about How to recognize a scam.

Change your MFA settings

After you sign in to your CRA account, on the Welcome page select Security settings then Multi-factor authentication to update your current MFA settings or enroll in additional MFA options. You can:

  • Add or change a phone number for MFA
  • Change your language settings
  • Add or change an MFA option
  • Generate a new passcode grid

If you can't receive your one-time passcode

If your phone number has changed and you can no longer receive your one-time passcode to that number, you can use another MFA option you are already enrolled with.

If you still cannot receive your one-time passcode, contact us.
